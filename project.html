Project Description

Research Background

‚Ä¢ BIOTECH CHALLENGE

Escherichia coli is one of the most widely used organisms for recombinant protein production, responsible for approximately one-third of approved therapeutic recombinant products.

‚Ä¢ TRANSLATION RHYTHM

Protein translation in E. coli does not proceed at a uniform rate. Translation elongation speed is strongly influenced by codon identity and tRNA abundance, creating a characteristic "translation rhythm" that critically affects protein yield and co-translational folding.

‚Ä¢ REDOX LIMITATION

The intrinsically reducing nature of the E. coli cytoplasm presents a major limitation for proteins requiring disulfide bonds for proper folding and function.

Project Rationale

‚Ä¢ EXISTING LIMITATIONS

Existing oxidative chassis (Origami, SHuffle) modify the global redox environment but lack precise control over translation kinetics and co-translational folding. They often severely perturb cellular homeostasis, leading to reduced growth rates and poor stability.

‚Ä¢ dogmaOS SOLUTION

dogmaOS addresses these limitations by establishing a systematic strategy for reprogramming protein expression through translation kinetics engineering, enabling production of proteins with stringent requirements for both translation dynamics and disulfide bond formation.

CYCLE 1

tRNA-Abundance-Controllable E. coli Chassis

01
Foundation Stage
Design ‚Üí Build ‚Üí Test 3/3 Complete

Design

Engineering an E. coli chassis with controllable tRNA abundance for selected tRNA families (Arg/Leu/Gly). Systematic screening to avoid lethality while achieving tunable translation kinetics.

Build

Using Œª-Red homologous recombination and CRISPR-Cas9 for precise tRNA gene deletion/modification. Constructing tRNA-abundance-controllable strains with validated genomic modifications.

Test

tRNA abundance measurement via qRT-PCR/tRNA-seq. Growth curve analysis. Translation efficiency assays using codon-enriched reporter systems. Model validation and refinement.

Key Innovation

Direct regulation of tRNA abundance provides an additional regulatory dimension beyond codon replacement, enabling fine-tuned control of translation kinetics without altering coding sequences.

Signal Peptide Engineering

  • Screening of optimal signal peptide sequences using molecular simulations
  • Regulation of signal peptide translation speed via synonymous codon substitution
  • Optimization of Sec pathway-mediated periplasmic targeting

Disulfide Bond Analysis

  • BPTI as model protein for disulfide bond formation studies
  • Molecular dynamics simulations to analyze translation rhythm effects
  • Synonymous codon variants for low/medium/high translation speeds

Integration Strategy

Combines translation kinetics control from Cycle 1 with the naturally oxidizing periplasmic environment, enabling coordinated optimization of protein folding across both temporal and spatial dimensions.

Scytovirin (SVN) Expression

üî¨ SVN is a naturally occurring antiviral protein from cyanobacteria containing five disulfide bonds, representing a structurally complex validation target.

Each disulfide bond is tested with low/medium/high translation speed variants to identify stage- and position-dependent requirements.

Final optimized SVN sequence integrates the best-performing translation rhythm strategies for each disulfide bond region.

Validation Methods

Qualitative detection of proper disulfide bond formation and folding

ELISA Analysis

Platform Scalability

Successful expression of SVN with its complex five-disulfide-bond structure demonstrates the scalability of the dogmaOS platform for producing structurally demanding proteins, validating it as a generalizable strategy for multi-disulfide protein expression.

Safety Considerations

Biosafety Level 1 Compliance

All experiments are conducted under Biosafety Level 1 (BSL-1) conditions using non-pathogenic Escherichia coli BL21 (DE3) strains.

Standard laboratory safety protocols are strictly followed, including proper waste disposal, sterilization procedures, and personal protective equipment (PPE) usage.

Regular safety training and equipment maintenance ensure a safe working environment for all team members.

Dual-Use Research Assessment

The project has been reviewed for dual-use potential in consultation with biosafety experts. All research outputs are intended for peaceful applications in biotechnology and medicine.

The platform is designed specifically for improving recombinant protein production efficiency and has no direct weapons-related applications.

Open communication about research goals and responsible innovation practices are maintained throughout the project.
